About Sulu

Welcome to the Province of Sulu

Sulu ([sʊˈlu]), officially the Province of Sulu (Tausūg: Wilāya sin Lupa' Sūg; Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Sulu), is a province of the Philippines in the Sulu Archipelago. Its capital is Jolo, located on the island of the same name, while Maimbung—once the royal capital of the Sultanate of Sulu—is also found within its borders.

Strategically situated along the southern edge of the Sulu Sea and the northern boundary of the Celebes Sea, Sulu is historically rich and culturally vibrant. Despite facing significant economic challenges, recent improvements have been noted, with poverty incidence dropping from 75.3% in 2018 to 51% in 2021.

Geography: Sulu covers a land area of 3,436.99 square kilometers (1,327.03 square miles) and is divided into 19 municipalities and 410 barangays, with a population density of approximately 240 inhabitants per square kilometer.

History & People: The province’s history is shaped by the arrival of Islam, the establishment of the Sultanate of Sulu, and centuries of resistance against colonial powers. The predominant Tausug people, whose name is derived from "tau" (person) and "suug" (old name for Jolo), continue to influence the cultural and political landscape of Sulu.
